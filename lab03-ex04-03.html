King Abdulaziz University (KAU) was founded in 1967 in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. In 2000/2001, it had 2,000 faculty and more than 37,000 students. Established initially as a private university by a group of businessmen led by Sheikh Muhammad Abu Bakr Bakhashab Pasha and including the writer Hamza Bogary. In 1974 King Abdulaziz University was converted to a public university by a resolution of the Council Ministers of Saudi Arabia. In 2016, it was ranked the top Arab university by Times Higher Education.
